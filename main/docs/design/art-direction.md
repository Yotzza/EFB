# Art Direction

The art direction is muted top-down 16-bit pixel survival.

## Visual Target

- Top-down or high three-quarter readable gameplay.
- Low-saturation, realistic palette.
- Post-apocalyptic Eastern Europe: concrete, cracked plaster, rusted metal, old apartment blocks, improvised shelters, damp roads, faded signs, utility poles, overgrown lots.
- Gritty but readable. Detail should not blur tile boundaries or gameplay silhouettes.
- Clean pixel edges with minimal anti-aliasing.
- Soft ambient lighting and restrained contrast.

## Avoid

- Glossy fantasy lighting.
- Neon-heavy palettes.
- Smooth vector-like edges.
- Overly saturated greens, purples, or blues.
- Modern clean sci-fi UI.
- Cartoon exaggeration that breaks the survival tone.

## Asset Rules

- Keep scale consistent across character, item, prop, and tile assets.
- Prefer tileable ground/wall assets for environments.
- Keep interactable objects visually distinct through silhouette and value, not bright saturation.
- UI buttons can be stylized, but should still feel worn and practical.

## New Asset Checklist

- Does it read at gameplay zoom?
- Does it fit the low-saturation palette?
- Does it preserve crisp pixel edges?
- Does it support the Eastern European post-apocalyptic setting?
- Is it referenced in `docs/product/feature-ledger.md` if it enables a new feature?
